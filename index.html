Strategy overview

Statistical signals, disciplined exits, real-world costs.

The strategy treats extreme short-term moves as candidates for reversion, then uses filters to avoid low-quality setups. The goal is not to predict every trend — it is to isolate repeatable price dislocations with asymmetric risk control.

01

Inefficiency hypothesis

Temporary crowding and liquidity imbalance can push perpetual prices away from their local equilibrium. The strategy enters after evidence of exhaustion, not at the start of a move.

02

Statistical filtering

Feature research compares winning and losing cohorts, then filters candidates using realized-volatility percentiles, market heatmap features, and a logistic model layer.

03

Execution-aware validation

Backtests are checked against fees and slippage, with live PNL tracked against BTC trend to detect whether the edge survives outside the research notebook.

Research process

From feature separation to deployable rules.

The research flow starts with explanatory features, validates them against outlier groups, then converts the signal into entry/exit logic that can be evaluated under realistic trading costs.

“The edge is not one indicator. It is the combination of a market anomaly, robust filtering, and refusing bad exits.”

✓Universe filter: listed assets older than four months
✓Signal filter: volatility percentile and heatmap features
✓Model layer: logistic filter to reduce false positives
✓Exit layer: MA7 second cross, take profit, and stop logic
1

Detect dislocation

Rank assets by short-term deviation and realized-volatility context to find stretched moves likely to mean revert.

2

Filter noisy candidates

Remove setups that look statistically similar to previous worst cases; keep candidates with stronger historical separation.

3

Backtest with cost assumptions

Evaluate the rules under different fee and slippage assumptions to see whether performance remains stable.

4

Monitor live drift

Compare live cumulative PNL against BTC trend and daily PNL distribution to catch edge decay or regime change.

Evidence & performance

Backtest, robustness, and live validation.

These charts summarize the research evidence. The focus is not just the final equity curve, but whether the signal survives filtering, costs, and real trading.

Filtered equity curves

The strategy was tested across multiple filtering stages: a listing-age universe filter, a heatmap-based filter, and a logistic-filtered version. The comparison makes regime-specific drawdowns visible instead of hiding them behind a single result.

Feature separation

The winner and worst-case cohorts show different distributions for rv168_p90, supporting its use as a filtering feature rather than a standalone trading signal.

Cost sensitivity

The sensitivity grid tests fees and slippage from 0 to 10 bps. Returns remain in a similar range while max drawdown stays close to four percent in the displayed scenarios.

In-sample report

The perpetuals report shows 78 closed trades, a 69.23% win rate, and an equity curve rising from roughly 100k to 180k in the displayed in-sample period. Exit analysis highlights the MA7 second-cross rule as the dominant exit condition.

Risk controls

Built to survive being wrong.

Mean reversion strategies can fail quickly during trend continuation. This project therefore emphasizes filters, exits, and monitoring rather than relying on a high win rate alone.
